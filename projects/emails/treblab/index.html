1. Do cross training
Jog and walk or combine running and riding a bike. Run for a short while and then stop to do some exercises to prevent getting out of breath.
2. Don't forget to breathe
Sounds ridiculous? Some people do it. They just hold their breath pressing on.
3. Don't run faster than you can
Keep the right path and do not be too hard on yourself.
4. Run and speak
It's the best test. If you can speak while running, the pace is right for you.
5. Use mouth to inhale
6. Watch for signs
Such things as nausea or dizziness are the symptoms of hypoxia.
7. Warm up well before you run
8. Drink up
Get enough of clean water while running.
9. Pick the right distance
10. Watch out for pain
If you get tired, it's ok, but if you feel pain, stop your training for the day.
